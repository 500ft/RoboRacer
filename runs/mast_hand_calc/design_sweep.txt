RECOMMENDATION
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
  Revised mast: 6061-T6 aluminum, L = 100 mm (from 120), OD = 20 mm (from 16), wall t = 1.5 mm (stock).
    Change vs baseline: L -20 mm, OD +4 mm, same wall, same material.
  -> f1 = 309.3 Hz  (>= 200 Hz guard; 1.55x the guard, 3.1x the 100 Hz control rate)  PASS
  -> crash-case strength SF = 7.04 (>= 1.5)  PASS   (sigma_crash = 39.2 MPa, delta = 0.190 mm)
  -> mast self-mass 23.5 g (baseline 22.1 g): a +1.4 g penalty.

  WHY it works: shortening L from 120 to 100 mm raises k by (120/100)^3 = 1.73x,
  and growing OD from 16 to 20 mm raises I by 2.07x; m_eff barely moves
  (tip mass dominates), so f1 jumps 163.8 -> 309.3 Hz. Strength improves too
  (more I lowers bending stress), so the fix is monotonic in both checks.

  CFRP note: the same 100 mm / 20 mm tube in CFRP would reach
  f1 = 374.7 Hz at 13.9 g (-9.6 g vs the Al rec) — lighter and
  stiffer, but anisotropic with no single yield, so aluminum is the recommended
  baseline. CFRP is the upgrade path if mast mass ever becomes binding.
